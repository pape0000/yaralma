YARALMA — Final User Journey & MVP Blueprint
Two Senegalese Families. Shared Values. Three Screens. A Tailored Schedule.
👨‍👧 THE CHARACTERS
The Muslim Family (Pikine, Dakar)
Demba (42): Merchant at Sandaga. Mouride. Worried about his son’s digital "Kersa" (modesty).
Pathé (9): Loves football and cartoons. Uses a shared tablet and the TCL Android TV.
The Christian Family (Ziguinchor, Casamance)
Joseph (44): Civil servant. Practicing Catholic. Concerned about Marie's exposure to foreign values.
Marie (10): Loves music and cooking videos. Uses her father’s Tecno phone and a Samsung Smart TV.
📍 PHASE 1 — THE "LINK" (Setup in < 5 Minutes)
Instead of building a new platform, YARALMA acts as a Protective Extension that links directly to existing accounts.
Step 1: The Six Pillars
Before any settings, both fathers see the foundation. Six words appear:
🤝 Teranga (Hospitality) | 🦁 Jom (Dignity) | 🙈 Kersa (Modesty)
🤫 Sutura (Discretion) | ⏳ Muñ (Patience) | ✨ Ngor (Integrity)
“These values belong to every Senegalese person. They guide YARALMA.”
Step 2: Faith Selection
Demba selects Islam (Mouride).
Joseph selects Christian.
The app logic shifts: Prayer times for Demba, Liturgical calendar for Joseph.
Step 3: Account Handshake (The MVP Magic)
YouTube: Demba signs in with Google. YARALMA uses the API to instantly disable "Shorts," hide the "Search" bar, and force "Explore Mode" on Pathé’s profile.
Netflix: Joseph follows a guided sync. YARALMA shows him exactly where to set the Profile PIN and which 47 titles (flagged by the local Christian community) to block for Marie.
📍 PHASE 2 — THE EXTENSION IN DAILY LIFE
The YARALMA extension sits as a silent "Overlay" on top of the YouTube and Netflix apps.
Scenario A: The Silent Blur (Sutura & Kersa)
Marie is watching a show. A scene becomes too explicit or violent.
The Tech: The extension detects the maturity metadata or community-flagged timestamps.
The Action: The screen softly blurs. A small 🦁 appears. No alarm sounds. The children keep scrolling/watching when the scene passes.
Value: Sutura protected. No shame, just protection.
Scenario B: The Holy Lock (Faith)
In Pikine (6:47 PM): Pathé’s tablet dims. A sunset sky appears. “Waktu Màgrib — Time for prayer. 🦁”
In Ziguinchor (Sunday 8:30 AM): Marie’s phone locks. “It is time for Mass. Blessings on your family. 🙏”
The Result: The devices respect the rhythm of the home automatically.
Scenario C: The Wolof Guardian
Pathé watches a Wolof drama. A character uses a vulgar insult.
The Tech: The "Wolof Ear" (Speech Recognition) identifies the root word.
The Action: The audio mutes for 3 seconds. Pathé learns that "Street Wolof" has no place in a house of Jom.
📍 PHASE 3 — THE PARENTAL REMOTE (WhatsApp)
The MVP uses WhatsApp as the dashboard, as both fathers are active there.
The Weekly Report (Every Sunday)
Report for Demba (Pathé)
Report for Joseph (Marie)
⏱ 4h 18min Total Time
⏱ 3h 52min Total Time
🛡️ 14 YouTube Shorts blocked
🛡️ 8 Netflix titles hidden
📿 35/35 Prayers respected
⛪ Sunday Mass lock honored
🦁 Jom & Muñ honored.
🦁 Ngor & Kersa honored.

Instant Control
If Demba is at the market and wants Pathé to stop:
Demba sends LOCK to the YARALMA WhatsApp Bot.
The Bot sends a signal to the Tablet extension.
Pathé sees: "Screens are locked. Goodnight Pathé. 🦁"
📍 PHASE 4 — THE "GROWING UP" TRANSITION
Two years later, Pathé and Marie turn 12. YARALMA sends a WhatsApp message:
"Your child is growing up. A person of Ngor is the same in public as in private. Talk to them about digital integrity."
YARALMA provides a Conversation Guide rooted in Senegalese proverbs to help the fathers move from "Filtering" to "Trust."
🌟 IN ONE SENTENCE
YARALMA encodes the values of the ancestors into the algorithms of the future, ensuring that every Senegalese child navigates the digital world with Jom and Ngor.
